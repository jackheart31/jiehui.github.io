My Journey

When I was 16, my impressions of coders were that of basement dwelling creatures, rarely creeping out of the house into the sunlight. They spend their time tinkering obsessively with a piece of metal box. When you pry the metal box away from them, they would screech loudly and begin to agressively attack you.

Yes, I did imagined Gollum.

Wait, so how did I end up being a coder myself?

I studied Physics in National University of Singapore (Yes, you can start steoreotyping me). There, I took up a coding course. Why not, I thought to myself. As coding has begun to expand its influence in current industries, surely it will be beneficial to add coding skills to my resume. There, my whole perspective of coding changed. I had assumed that coding was some obscure language that only Gollum was ever interested in. It probably had its own syntax and jargon and what not and who would even want to get into that?

Yet, my first coding experience blew me away. It exudes an elegance of logic which is pretty much lost in physics. Its syntax is merely an expression, a communication, a language. Soon, I began getting more and more into coding. I was particularly interested in algorithms. It was intense, thinking of various solutions to a problem, wondering which approaches would result in better times.

When I graduated, I was offered a job from UST Global. It was titled "RPA Developer". I had never heard anything like that in my entire life. So I did what I had to and went for the interview. I passed it and began my RPA journey.

Me getting my photo taken for my first lanyard!


From there, I began to learn more codes and RPA (Robotic process automation) which is apparently a very hot thing in IT. I am now deployed to Singtel where I help various employees by automating their workflows.
